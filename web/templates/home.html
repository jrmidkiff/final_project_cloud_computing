How the GAS Works

Annotation is a process by which a sequenced genome sample is analyzed to identify the locations of genes and all of the coding regions in a genome, and determine what those genes do. The GAS attaches “notes” to the genome sequence so that it may be interpreted by downstream analysis tools (or really smart humans).
